Scammer Tactics Timeline

Explore how crypto scams have evolved since Bitcoin's inception

2009
Early Bitcoin Era

2009-2011: The First Crypto Scams

Predominant Tactics:

  • Basic phishing emails claiming to be from "Satoshi Nakamoto"
  • Fake Bitcoin generators and miners
  • Simple Ponzi schemes promising Bitcoin returns

Insight: Early crypto scams were relatively unsophisticated, targeting early adopters with technical backgrounds.

Exchange Emergence

2012-2013: Exchange Vulnerabilities

Predominant Tactics:

  • Exchange hacks targeting weak security
  • Fake exchanges mimicking legitimate platforms
  • Wallet theft through malware

Insight: As exchanges became popular, they became prime targets for sophisticated attacks.

Altcoin Boom

2014-2016: The Rise of Altcoin Scams

Predominant Tactics:

  • Pump and dump schemes with new altcoins
  • Fake altcoin projects with copied whitepapers
  • Mining pool scams

Insight: The proliferation of alternative cryptocurrencies created new opportunities for scammers to create worthless tokens.

ICO Mania

2017-2018: ICO Fraud Explosion

Predominant Tactics:

  • Fraudulent ICOs with false promises
  • Celebrity-endorsed scam projects
  • Exit scams after successful fundraising

Insight: The ICO boom created a gold rush mentality, allowing scammers to raise millions before disappearing.

DeFi Genesis

2019-2020: Early DeFi Exploits

Predominant Tactics:

  • Flash loan attacks on DeFi protocols
  • Rug pulls on decentralized exchanges
  • Smart contract vulnerabilities exploitation

Insight: DeFi introduced complex attack vectors requiring technical expertise to execute but offering potentially larger payouts.

NFT & Metaverse

2021-2022: NFT & Metaverse Scams

Predominant Tactics:

  • Fake NFT marketplaces and collections
  • Discord hacks targeting NFT communities
  • Counterfeit NFTs and artificial price manipulation

Insight: NFTs created new opportunities for scammers, targeting both creators and collectors with sophisticated social engineering.

AI & Cross-Chain

2023-2024: AI-Enhanced Attacks

Predominant Tactics:

  • AI-generated deepfake videos for crypto scams
  • Cross-chain bridge exploits
  • Advanced phishing using LLM-powered social engineering

Insight: AI tools have enabled scammers to create highly convincing fake content and target users across multiple blockchains.

Future Threats

2025+: Next Generation Scams to Watch For

Predicted Tactics:

  • Quantum computing attacks on cryptographic protocols
  • Identity theft via compromised biometric wallets
  • Sophisticated "sleeper" smart contracts in legitimate projects

Insight: Future scammers will likely exploit emerging technologies while developing increasingly patient, sophisticated approaches.
