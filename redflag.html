Cryptocurrency Red Flag Detector

Evaluate the risk level of your cryptocurrency investment by answering a few simple questions.

Step 1: Select a cryptocurrency below to begin the assessment.

Question 1 of 10
Does the investment promise guaranteed returns or profits?
Consider if they advertise specific percentage returns with certainty

Major Red Flag: Guaranteed Returns

No legitimate investment can guarantee profits, especially high returns. All investments carry risk, and guarantees of specific returns are a hallmark of scams and Ponzi schemes.

Red Flag: Return Guarantees

Even moderate guaranteed returns should be viewed with suspicion. Legitimate investments acknowledge market risks and avoid making promises about specific returns.

Question 2 of 10
Is there a sense of urgency or limited time to invest?
Are they pressuring you to act quickly to avoid missing out?

Major Red Flag: Extreme Urgency

Creating artificial time pressure is a classic manipulation tactic used by scammers to prevent you from researching properly or consulting with others. Legitimate investments don't disappear overnight.

Red Flag: Time Pressure

While some legitimate offerings may have limited availability, excessive emphasis on scarcity or impending price changes is often used to rush decisions and bypass careful evaluation.

Question 3 of 10
Is there a clear explanation of how the investment generates returns?
Can you understand the business model or technology behind it?

Major Red Flag: Lack of Transparency

When an investment can't clearly explain how it generates returns, it's often because there is no legitimate mechanism. Vague references to proprietary algorithms or advanced technology without specifics are common in scams.

Red Flag: Insufficient Information

Partial explanations that leave out critical details about how money is actually made should raise concerns. Legitimate projects can explain their value proposition clearly, even if some technical aspects are complex.

Question 4 of 10
Who is promoting this investment opportunity?
Consider the credibility of the individuals or organization behind it

Major Red Flag: Anonymous or Fake Team

Legitimate projects have real team members who are publicly identifiable and accountable. Anonymous teams or stolen identities are extremely common in crypto scams.

Red Flag: Paid Promotions

Heavy reliance on celebrity endorsements or influencer marketing without substantial discussion of the technology or business model is concerning. Many influencers are paid for promotion without proper disclosure.

Question 5 of 10
Does the project have a clear, detailed whitepaper or documentation?
Look for technical documentation that explains how the project works

Major Red Flag: Lack of Documentation

Legitimate crypto projects provide detailed technical documentation. Absence of a whitepaper or minimal information suggests there may be no real technology behind the project.

Red Flag: Problematic Documentation

Low-quality documentation with errors, plagiarized content, or grand claims without technical substance is a warning sign of an unprofessional or fraudulent project.

Question 6 of 10
Has the project's code been audited by reputable security firms?
Security audits help identify vulnerabilities and validate functionality

Major Red Flag: No Code Audits

Without security audits and publicly viewable code, there's no way to verify the project functions as claimed or is free from vulnerabilities that could lead to loss of funds.

Red Flag: Questionable Audits

Vague claims about audits without verifiable reports or audits from unknown firms provide little assurance. Legitimate projects provide transparent audit documentation.

Question 7 of 10
What is the token distribution structure?
Consider how tokens are allocated among team, investors, and users

Major Red Flag: Centralized Token Ownership

When a large percentage of tokens is controlled by founders with no lockup period, there's high risk of a "rug pull" where insiders sell their holdings all at once, crashing the price.

Red Flag: Unclear Token Distribution

Lack of transparency about token allocation or overly complex distribution schemes often hide inequitable arrangements that benefit insiders at the expense of regular investors.

Question 8 of 10
How is the investment opportunity being marketed?
Consider the marketing tactics and messaging used

Major Red Flag: Get-Rich-Quick Marketing

Marketing that focuses primarily on wealth generation rather than technological innovation or real-world utility is characteristic of scams that prey on FOMO (fear of missing out).

Red Flag: Misleading Comparisons

Claims like "the next Bitcoin" or unrealistic projections based on past crypto success stories are misleading marketing tactics designed to create unrealistic expectations.

Question 9 of 10
Is there a functioning product or just a concept?
Check if there's a working prototype or only promises

Major Red Flag: No Working Product

Projects collecting investment without any working product or clear development milestones carry significant risk. Ideas are common, but execution is what creates value.

Red Flag: Consistently Delayed Development

Repeated delays or a minimal product that doesn't match promised functionality suggests potential development issues or misleading claims about capabilities.

Question 10 of 10
Can you withdraw your investment easily if needed?
Consider any lockup periods or withdrawal restrictions

Major Red Flag: Trapped Investments

When a project makes it extremely difficult to withdraw funds, this is often a sign of a Ponzi scheme or exit scam. Legitimate investments allow reasonable liquidity.

Red Flag: Excessive Withdrawal Barriers

High fees or unreasonable waiting periods for withdrawals can be used to discourage investors from taking out their money, often to maintain artificial price stability or delay a collapse.
